Case Study: What is driving my NAV?

BizCo, a company dedicated to managing venture capital investments, is recently having some issues. Following the exit of all the members of the Executive Committee (ExCo), who left the company to join KKR, the Board of Directors (BoD) of BizCo appointed a new ExCo composed of Santi, Ainhoa, and Unai. It is January 10th, 2025.

The BoD needs to understand the dynamics of the Net Asset Value (NAV) of the portfolio, how this NAV evolved during 2024, and see if there are some estimates on what the NAV of the portfolio might look like as of the end of 2024 and as of today. In addition to this, it is key to understand what the underlying drivers are that explain the NAV and Money On Invested Capital (MOIC) dynamics of the portfolio, and the different clusters explaining it.

The ExCo is new to the role and needs to carry out this analysis for the meeting scheduled with the BoD on Friday 24th, 2025. The ExCo calls you, with a background in Data Analysis, to help them out with this task and provides you with a spreadsheet with the information of the portfolio at the portfolio company level (see attached) and some public market data.

The spreadsheet has different qualitative fields (e.g., report date, report currency, manager, fund, vertical, company name, technology, business model, investment status, round, current round, investment instrument) and quantitative fields (e.g., #shares, Last Price / Share, NAV, Investment). The info is at the investment level, so there might be many investments carried out on the same company at different points in time and valuations. Also, there are some listed companies on public markets among the portfolio companies.

You need to decide the type of analysis and outputs you will build in order to meet the needs of BizCoâ€™s BoD. You are free to ask as many questions as required, that will be responded to at least on a daily basis by the ExCo.

The ExCo will arrange a meeting with you on Wednesday 22nd or Thursday 23rd, where you will be presenting how you developed the analysis, the main conclusions of it, and the outputs generated.

Go for it!!!

Management and Investment Distribution Insights

Sector Investment Distribution

Financial Services is the clear leader in sector investment, followed by Business Services and Energy. Other sectors, like Agriculture, Healthtech, and Mobility & Transportation, contribute moderately, while areas like Climate Software, Gaming, and Cybersecurity represent the smallest shares.

Management Distribution

Towercarbon leads with a significantly larger distribution compared to other entities. Gospel and Platino also show strong representation.

Vertical Fund Distribution

Decarbonization and Growth funds dominate, both receiving nearly equal and substantial investment. FinTech is the next notable category.

Technology Overview

AI & ML is the largest category, receiving 12.3% of the investments, highlighting its critical importance. Other significant categories include Data Analytics (9.59%) and Green Hydrogen (7.53%), reflecting strong trends in analytics and renewable energy technologies. A wide range of other technologies, such as Renewable Energy, FinTech, and Carbon Capture, are present but receive smaller proportions of investment.

This distribution shows a focus on cutting-edge and sustainable technologies, with a broad diversification across various domains.

Customer Relationship Model Overview

B2B is the predominant model, accounting for 41.6% of the share, emphasizing the importance of business-to-business relationships. Growth & Early and B2C follow, contributing 36.5% and 15.5%, respectively. Models such as B2B2C and Other have minimal representation, indicating secondary or niche strategies.

The focus on B2B and Growth & Early models aligns with strategies aimed at scalability and enterprise-level operations.

Business/Revenue Model Overview

SaaS is the dominant model, representing 52.2%, underscoring its widespread adoption in generating revenue. Marketplace (15.5%) and On-demand (12.7%) models also hold significant shares, reflecting their relevance in modern digital platforms. Other models, such as Free-Model, Licenses, and Subscription, make up smaller proportions, indicating their niche roles.

This distribution highlights a strong preference for scalable and recurring revenue models like SaaS and Marketplace.
